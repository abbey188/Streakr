The Squad Room

A private conversation space per group. Match events and your squad's messages share one stream — the game shouts in colour, people talk calm. Every choice below is drawn from Streakr's own visual language, so it reads as if it was always here.

Mockup renders with system fonts; production keeps Space Grotesk + JetBrains Mono. Colours, spacing, radii and treatments are the app's real tokens.

See a live squad before you open it

An unread count sits on each group in the main list, so a room that's buzzing pulls you in — you don't have to open every group to find where the talk is. Tapping through lands you on the Squad Room tab.

Why · the pull-back only works if you can see it from outside

One avatar everywhere: the mascot

Every row — message and match event — is led by the member's mascot, upper-body: the exact same AvatarRenderer already used on the leaderboard and in the Inbox. No stray emoji tiles, no second avatar system. The Squad Room instantly belongs to Streakr.

Why · uniformity with what exists > a new look; reuses the pipeline

The contrast: a coloured edge, not a loud card

Since every row is mascot-led, event-vs-message rides on a thin coloured edge in the margin — no tinted fill. A match event gets a stripe (🔥 orange, 💀 red, 👑 amber) and the type emoji inline; a message is a plain bubble. The room reads as a chat with the game noted quietly at the edge, not a feed you talk over.

Why · you chose calm — the game punctuates, it doesn't dominate

Left-aligned, never "mine on the right"

Everything is left-aligned, Discord-style — the stream isn't only your messages. A WhatsApp right/left split breaks the moment a system event, which belongs to no one, lands between two of your lines.

Why · the room owns the timeline, not you

One level deep, and that's the rule

Replies indent once under their parent with a thin rail and smaller avatars. No nesting past that — deeper threads are unreadable on a phone and turn a squad chat into a forum, which is the public feed's job, not this one.

Why · light threading keeps banter legible; infinite nesting doesn't

A prompt, not a void

A new group has nothing to show. Rather than blank space, it names the two ways the room fills — you talk, or the game posts for you — so the value is obvious before anyone types.

Why · the empty app is where most social features die

Composer · pinned

One line, always reachable

Anchored to the bottom, panel-filled to match every other input in the app, orange send. No formatting bar, no attachments — a squad room is for quick lines, not composed posts. That restraint is the whole "not another posting app" stance, in one control.

Why · the input's simplicity sets the tone for what gets written

Replies ping. Reactions stay silent.

A reply to your message earns a push and an Inbox row, deep-linking straight into the Squad Room — this is the return-visit engine. It reuses the exact push path shipped this week and a new Squad toggle in Settings, so anyone can mute it.

Reactions deliberately send nothing. A ping on every 🔥 is how you teach people to turn a group off.

Why · "someone replied to you" is the most reliable reason anyone reopens an app

Design decisions — locked

✓ Mascot avatars everywhere
The upper-body AvatarRenderer already used on the leaderboard and Inbox, on every row — message and event alike. No second avatar system.
✓ Quiet events — coloured edge + type chip, wins glow / breaks fade
No filled cards. Every event carries a thin coloured stripe and a small type chip (🔥 Streak · 👑 Crown · 💀 Broken) so it's never mistaken for a message. Emotion is reserved for the extremes — a crown glows amber, a break recedes; a streak sits neutral between.
✓ Message bubbles
Rounded panel bubbles for the warmer, more obviously-chat feel.
✓ Entry: tab in a group + unread on the Groups list
A Squad Room tab beside Leaderboard, and an unread count on each group in the main list so a live squad pulls you in from outside.
✓ Group-scoped · async threaded · reply pings, reactions silent
Private per group, polls like the app, one level of replies. A reply notifies you (new Squad pref); reactions never do.
